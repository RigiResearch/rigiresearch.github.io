Self-Adaptation and Runtime Model Evolution Toward Next-Generation Digital Twins


Description

Advanced digital twins (DTs) will enable the convergence of physical and virtual worlds by complementing associated services or functions existing in the former with augmented reasoning and control capabilities in the latter. These advanced, next-generation DTs will allow businesses and practitioners implementing them to reduce operational complexity, cope with real-world uncertainties, and generate value automatically thanks to the exchange of accumulated knowledge. Our contributions are twofold. First, investigate a reference framework that leverages research progress in dynamic context management, runtime models and adaptive control, as well as feedback loops for self-adaptation and management to enable the transition of current third-stage DTs to the fourth one. Second, we coduct research in regards the progression toward the fifth stage, introducing a novel mechanism based on adaptive experimentation to guide the evolution of DTs. We are currently applying our reference framework to relevant scenarios in smart transit and personalized healthcare. This includes the development of adaptive control and self-adaptative/management mechanisms for the continuous evolution of DT models.
